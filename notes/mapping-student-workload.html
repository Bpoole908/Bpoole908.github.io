Keep Teaching: Mapping Student Workload for Online Activities and Assessments

This workshop was focused on a technique called activity mapping. The goal of activity mapping is to visually map out your activities for the semester. Activity mapping allows professors to visualize the minimum (best case scenario) and maximum (worst case scenario) amount time it will take a student to complete the work for a given week. This is can also be done for the professor to assess the minimum and maximum amount of time to create the work for the week. You also don't have to do weeks you can generalize to longer time periods.

The visualization works by plotting five main categories: instructor communication, information resource, practice, dialogue, and feedback. On top of theses five main categories the time to complete said category should also be include. You can also account for categories that might be revisited throughout the week - increasing the time it takes to complete the weekly workload.
